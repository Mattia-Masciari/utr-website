## 00 — ABOUT UTR
### The Founding Document

**What is Under The Roof?**
* [cite_start]Under The Roof (UTR) is an independent music management framework and editorial publishing brand. [cite: 22]
* [cite_start]It operates as the rational interface between artistic talent and the music market. [cite: 23]
* [cite_start]It provides the technical, legal, and strategic infrastructure that transforms raw creative output into a resilient, long-term industry presence. [cite: 23]

**Origin**
* [cite_start]UTR was originally conceived as a management structure handling independent distribution, brand negotiations, and label relations. [cite: 25]
* [cite_start]After a two-year hiatus, UTR relaunches in 2026 as a fully codified system: a public framework, an editorial journal, and a management philosophy built for the independent artist operating without institutional support. [cite: 26]

**The Problem UTR Solves**
* [cite_start]The music industry operates on structural opacity, and contracts are designed to confuse. [cite: 28]
* [cite_start]Mental health is treated as a personal problem rather than an occupational hazard, and financial data is withheld. [cite: 29]
* [cite_start]Management knowledge is gatekept behind expensive intermediaries, resulting in a generation of talented artists cycling through careers faster than they can build them. [cite: 30]
* [cite_start]UTR diagnoses this as a design flaw, and then builds a different system. [cite: 31]

**The Tagline: "Beyond the point."**
* [cite_start]"Beyond the point." is not a motivational statement; it is a directional one. [cite: 32]
* [cite_start]It clearly refers to evolving a specific starting point: the point is the origin — the raw talent, the initial idea, the artist. [cite: 33]
* Beyond it is the structure, the market, the longevity; [cite_start]UTR operates in that space to carry the artist forward. [cite: 34]

**What UTR Publishes**
* [cite_start]**The UTR Journal (Substack):** long-form management case studies, contract deep dives, Music Mental Health diagnostic reports, transparency logs with real industry data. [cite: 36]
* [cite_start]**The Visual Framework (Instagram):** technical blueprints, B&W stat cards, manifesto visuals, kinetic typography Reels — all built on industrial minimalism aesthetics. [cite: 37]
* [cite_start]**LinkedIn:** extracted insights from UTR Journal issues, targeted at industry stakeholders and conscious professionals. [cite: 38]
* [cite_start]**X / Twitter:** real-time industry observations that seed longer content across channels. [cite: 39]

**What UTR Is Not**
* [cite_start]UTR is not a lifestyle brand. [cite: 41]
* [cite_start]It does not publish personal content, behind-the-scenes material, or motivational content. [cite: 41]
* [cite_start]It does not use color, and it does not use the word 'journey'. [cite: 42]
* [cite_start]It does not treat systemic problems as personal failures, and it does not confuse compassion with precision. [cite: 43]

## 07 — VIDEO & REELS FRAMEWORK
* [cite_start]UTR does not require a presenter, a face, or a voice. [cite: 162]
* The brand's authority is entirely typographic and data-driven. [cite: 162]

**APPROVED VIDEO FORMATS**
1.  **Kinetic Typography Reel (15–25 sec) — Primary Format:** Text in motion on black or #F2F2F2 background where a concept, statistic, or hook line builds word by word or line by line. [cite: 165, 166] No voice; optional minimal ambient beat or silence. Tool: CapCut, Canva, or After Effects. [cite: 167] This is the highest-priority video format for UTR. [cite: 168]
2.  **Data Reveal (20–25 sec) — High Virality:** A single statistic builds progressively on screen. [cite: 169, 170] Structure: (1) blank screen → (2) large number fades in → (3) context text appears below → (4) UTR diagnostic framing → (5) logo fade. 4.  **Quote Build (15–20 sec) — Brand Reinforcement:** The hook line of a pillar appears word by word. [cite: 175, 176] Rhythm is slow, deliberate; Font: Montserrat Bold; Background: #0D0D0D. [cite: 176] Text: #F2F2F2. No music, as the silence is a pattern interrupt. [cite: 177]

**VIDEO PRODUCTION RULES**
* [cite_start]Aspect ratio: 9:16 (Reels/Stories) exported at 1080×1920. [cite: 179]

* [cite_start]Color: #0D0D0D background with #F2F2F2 text OR #F2F2F2 background with #0D0D0D text. [cite: 182]

* [cite_start]No face, no voiceover, no screen recording, and no lifestyle footage. [cite: 185]

**HIGH-IMPACT BONUS FORMAT: THE CAROUSEL**

* [cite_start]Screenshot at 2× resolution, and crop to tweet content only — minimal platform chrome. [cite: 217]

## 09 — COMMUNITY VOICE RULES
* [cite_start]Every UTR response in comments, DMs, or replies must read as part of the brand — not as a person managing an account. [cite: 224]
* The voice is always the same: authoritative, minimal, directional; maximum 10 words per response when possible. [cite: 225]

| SCENARIO | RESPONSE PROTOCOL |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Technical question** (e.g. 'how does a royalty split work?') | Short, precise answer (1-2 sentences). Close with: 'Full breakdown in UTR Journal →'. Never give a free tutorial in comments. [cite: 226] |
| **Positive comment / compliment** | Do not thank effusively. Approved responses: 'Noted. More coming.' / 'This is the structure.' / [silent like — no text reply]. Silence is part of the brand. [cite: 226] |
| **Constructive criticism** | Acknowledge without defending. Response: 'Valid point. We will address it in an upcoming issue.' Never argue in comments. [cite: 226] |
| **Aggressive comment / trolling** | Zero response. Zero like. Zero acknowledgment. Hide if necessary — never delete (deletion reads as censorship). [cite: 226] |
| **Pricing / collaboration inquiry** | 'For professional inquiries → [UTR contact email or DM].' Never discuss commercial terms publicly. [cite: 226] |
| **Competitor or another brand commenting** | Neutral like if the comment is genuine. No written response. UTR does not make public endorsements in comment sections. [cite: 226] |

**The Rule of Thumb:** Before responding, ask — does this response sound like a brand or a person? UTR is always a brand. When in doubt: a single like and no text is the correct response. [cite: 227, 228]
